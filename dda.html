New Feature Experience

Research to power an external partnership that brought new features to our platforms.

Co-lead researcher · 6 weeks · Qualitative research

Context

The company was considering taking on a new vendor to provide a white-labeled experience with a variety of features. Stakeholders needed to understand the usability and branding strengths.

Research would help the two companies make decisions on their partnership. The evaluative results were intended to provide insights on where my company should push for design customization.

Research Goal: Uncover how our customers interact with a proposed new feature in order to inform a vendor partnership.

Constraints

  • The vendor templates were limited on design changes
  • My company’s platforms had technological and implementation limitations

Approach

  • The complexity of the requirements led me to recommend and conduct in-depth interviews
  • I partnered with a designer to build a medium fidelity prototype to capture the possible integration between our platforms and the new feature

Key Findings

  • Our customers identified the experience as “belonging” to our company. They were able to successfully locate and use the new features.
  • Participants expected to locate a certain vendor feature where they typically use a similar bank feature.
  • One of the features was not intuitive due to the layout design.

Outcomes

The success of the feature led to feature adoption and a lasting company partnership.

Our findings led to productive discussions with the vendor. They were able to accommodate design changes around many of the design limitations we documented.

Due to technical constraints, the company was not able to implement a location for the feature that would meet the participant expectations in our study. We are intending to track customer feedback upon release.

Reflection

This study reminded me of an important lesson: sometimes you have to compromise on an idealized user experience to get a product to release.
