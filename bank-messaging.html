Bank Messaging

How do our communications build trust or trigger suspicion?

Lead Researcher · 12 weeks · Interviews & quantitative testing

The Need

The experience design, marketing, and fraud departments were looking to increase digital customer engagement by fostering credibility in our messages. The core question was how our communications could prevent harm without eroding trust.

Why It Mattered

Poorly timed warnings risked both user harm and disengagement. Over-warning could train users to ignore critical signals, while under-warning exposed them to financial loss.

Constraints & Tradeoffs

  • Limited time before regulatory review
  • No historical behavioral data on scam exposure
  • Could not run live A/B tests at this stage

Method Selection

We needed to understand emotional reactions and mental models, not optimize copy. We chose early concept testing with interviews to surface thresholds of concern and language sensitivity.

What We Found

  • Users distinguished between “alerts” and “education”
  • Timing mattered more than wording
  • Overly authoritative language reduced perceived agency

Outcome & Impact

The team shifted from persistent alerts to contextual education. Messaging guidelines were adopted across future security features.

Reflection

This study reinforced how easily well-intentioned safeguards can undermine trust when emotional context is ignored.
